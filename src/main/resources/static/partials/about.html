LINCS Proteomics Landing Page

LINCS proteomics portal has been developed by the LINCS Data Coordination and Integration Center (DCIC) in collaboration with other LINCS centers, and with the goal of facilitating the navigation through and interaction with the LINCS proteomics data. Your feedback is highly appreciated and please contact our team using this link.

The LINCS-Proteomics portal aims to:

  Facilitate navigation, search, and interpretation of LINCS proteomics data using alternative “data-centric” and “assay-centric” lens or views
  Provide a guide to LINCS Data Portal other resources developed by LINCS-DCIC to facilitate finding and downloading proteomic data
  Provide intuitive representations of relationship between protein entities that are measured as the snapshot of the biological state provided by each method/assay
  Provide links to other resources and tools for proteomics, in particular those developed as part of the LINCS consortium

The Library of Integrated Network-Based Cellular Signatures (LINCS)

LINCS program aims to create a network-based understanding of biology by cataloging changes in gene expression and other cellular processes that occur when cells are exposed to a variety of perturbing agents.

This list includes data generating and data processing centers

HMS LINCS

Harvard Medical School

The HMS LINCS Center collects multiplex perturbagen-response data across a number of complementary experimental platforms including live- and fixed-cell imaging, sandwich immunoassays, protein mass spectrometry, and mRNA profiling as a means to measure the levels.

LINCS DToxS

Icahn School of Medicine

The Drug Toxicity Signature Generation Center is a Systems Pharmacology research center at the Icahn School of Medicine. The proteomics experiments are conducted at the Center for Advanced Proteomics, Rutgers-New Jersey Medical School.

NeuroLINCS

University of California- Irvine, Cedars Sinai, the Gladstone Institute, MIT, and Johns Hopkins University

The NeuroLINCS Center studies ALS and SMA by characterizing the molecular networks within patient-derived iPSCs, and their differentiated progeny, across many regulatory layers.

LINCS PCCSE

Broad Institute

The LINCS Proteomic Characterization Center for Signaling and Epigenetics at the Broad Institute/MIT/University of Washington utilizes the P100 and GCP assays to profile the response of human cell lines to perturbations.

BD2K-LINCS DCIC

Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai, University of Cincinnati and University of Miami

The BD2K-LINCS Data Coordination and Integration Centers at Icahn School of Medicine, university of Miami and university of Cincinnati are responsible for harmonizing LINCS data with other available resources.

Tools to help you be more productive with LINCS and Non-LINCS proteomics datasets. For complete list of LINCS Workflows and Tools click here

PIR (The Protein Information Resource) is an integrated public bioinformatics resource to support genomic, proteomic and systems biology research and scientific studies.
piNet can be used to annotate, map and analyze a set of peptide moieties (including post-translationally modified peptides) identified by either targeted (SRM type) or SWATH (discovery) proteomic assays...
Panorama Repository Software for Targeted Proteomics Assays from Skyline.
iLINCS (Integrative LINCS) is an integrative web platform for analysis of LINCS data and signatures. The portal provides biologists-friendly user interfaces for analyzing transcriptomics and proteomics LINCS datasets...
Enrichr is sn intuitive web-based gene list enrichment analysis tool for inferring knowledge about an input gene set by comparing it to annotated gene sets representing prior biological knowledge with enrichment analysis.
piLINCS has been developed as part of the BD2K-LINCS Data Coordination and Integration Center to provide interactive and API access to proteomics data generated by the PCCSE center.
PhosphoSitePlus Freely accessible on-line systems biology resource devoted to commonly studied protein post-translational modifications...
HMS LINCS Database The Database contains all publicly available HMS LINCS datasets and information for each dataset about experimental reagents and experimental and data analysis protocols....
LINCS DATA PORTAL Provides unified interface for searching all LINCS dataset packages and entities.
PRO (PRotein Ontology) provides an ontological representation of protein-related entities by explicitly defining them and showing the relationships between them.
iPTMnet is a bioinformatics resource for integrated understanding of protein post-translational modifications (PTMs) in systems biology context.
iProLINK (integrated Protein Literature, INformation and Knowledge) is a resource with access to text mining tools and annotated corpora developed in house.
